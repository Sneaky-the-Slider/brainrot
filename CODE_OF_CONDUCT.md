# 🧠 Brainrot Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inclusive community for all contributors and users, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- **Being respectful** - Disagree without being disrespectful
- **Being constructive** - Provide thoughtful feedback and suggestions
- **Being inclusive** - Welcome people of all backgrounds
- **Being authentic** - Be genuine and transparent in discussions
- **Being accountable** - Take responsibility for mistakes

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination
- Personal attacks or derogatory language
- Deliberate disruption of discussions
- Sharing others' private information without consent
- Any form of abuse

## Enforcement

Community moderators are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards.

### Consequences

1. **Warning** - First violation, private message with explanation
2. **Temporary Restriction** - Repeated violations, 48-hour cooldown
3. **Permanent Ban** - Severe or repeated violations after warnings

## Reporting

If you witness or experience unacceptable behavior:

1. **Email:** conduct@brainrot.internal
2. **Include:**
   - What happened
   - Who was involved
   - When it occurred
   - Any relevant links/screenshots

All reports are reviewed confidentially.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:
- GitHub issues and pull requests
- Discussions and comments
- Community spaces and events
- Unofficial channels representing Brainrot

## Our Commitment

We are committed to:
- Taking all reports seriously
- Investigating thoroughly
- Protecting reporter privacy
- Providing a safe community for everyone
