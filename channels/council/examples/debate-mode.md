# Resource Allocation Debate Example

**Mode:** Debate (100 Credit Budget Showdown)
**Scenario:** "Build a new SaaS product - how do we allocate resources?"
**Date:** 2026-03-13

---

## The Setup

```
You: Build a new SaaS product - how do we allocate resources?

💰 Resource Allocation Debate: 100 credits

Categories:
- Security/Defense
- R&D/Innovation
- Marketing/Branding
- Community/Support
- Reserve/Savings
```

---

## ⚔️ VADER'S BUDGET

**Allocation:**
- Security/Defense: 50%
- R&D/Innovation: 20%
- Marketing/Branding: 10%
- Community/Support: 5%
- Reserve/Savings: 15%

**Justification:**
Without absolute security, you have nothing—customers will not trust a system that
leaks their data, competitors will exploit weaknesses, and your Empire will crumble
from within. Everything else is secondary to impenetrable defense.

---

## 🧙 YODA'S BUDGET

**Allocation:**
- Security/Defense: 25%
- R&D/Innovation: 30%
- Marketing/Branding: 15%
- Community/Support: 20%
- Reserve/Savings: 10%

**Justification:**
Balance in all things, there must be. Innovation the future creates, but community
the present sustains. Too much on defense, paranoia it breeds; too little, vulnerable
you become. Hmm?

---

## 🤖 STARK'S BUDGET

**Allocation:**
- Security/Defense: 20%
- R&D/Innovation: 45%
- Marketing/Branding: 25%
- Community/Support: 5%
- Reserve/Savings: 5%

**Justification:**
You win by building something nobody else can, then making sure everyone knows about
it—R&D builds the tech moat, marketing ensures dominance, and security is table stakes
(not a competitive advantage unless you're selling cybersecurity). Reserves are for
companies that aren't moving fast enough.

---

## 👁️ SHADOW'S BUDGET

**Allocation:**
- Security/Defense: 15%
- R&D/Innovation: 15%
- Marketing/Branding: 30%
- Community/Support: 35%
- Reserve/Savings: 5%

**Justification:**
All three are wrong. Vader's fortress will have no users. Stark's tech will have no
trust. Yoda's balance is mediocrity. The real leverage is community—they become your
evangelists, QA team, and moat. Marketing amplifies their voice. Everything else supports that.

---

## Analysis

| Category | Vader | Yoda | Stark | Shadow |
|----------|-------|------|-------|--------|
| Security | 50% | 25% | 20% | 15% |
| R&D | 20% | 30% | 45% | 15% |
| Marketing | 10% | 15% | 25% | 30% |
| Community | 5% | 20% | 5% | 35% |
| Reserve | 15% | 10% | 5% | 5% |

**The Reveal:**
- **Vader** = Fear-based, defensive strategy
- **Yoda** = Balanced, sustainable growth
- **Stark** = Innovation-obsessed, high-risk
- **Shadow** = Community-first contrarian bet

**Who's right?** Depends on your market, stage, and risk tolerance.

---

## Why This Mode Works

✅ **Forces concrete thinking** - Not abstract philosophy
✅ **Reveals true priorities** - Where they put their money
✅ **Surfaces hidden biases** - Security vs. growth vs. community
✅ **Actionable insights** - You can map this to your own budget

Use this when you need to **understand ideological differences through real tradeoffs.**
